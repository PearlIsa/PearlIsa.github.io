CABS Report for the School of Management

In this project, I utilized the Dimcli library in Python to query and mine data from the Dimensions.ai website. This data was then analyzed using R and presented with Quarto. I also I developed an interactive dashboard using R and Shiny to benchmark and analyze university research outputs. The dashboard provides a detailed comparison of selected universities based on several metrics such as citations, altmetric scores and contributions to Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

The aim was to generate a comprehensive report on the University of Bradford's publications in CABS-listed (Chartered Association of Business Schools) journals since 2018 till date. This involved examining publication trends, journal rankings, citation data, and researcher profiles. By integrating data from various sources and performing detailed analyses, we provided valuable insights into the research outputs and their impact.

Methodology:

Data Collection:

  • Queried the Dimensions database using research IDs from the staff list to collect publication data specific to the University.
  • Utilized the Dimcli library in Python to query and mine data from the Dimensions.ai website.
  • Loaded and processed data from CSV files using the here and readr libraries.

Data Analysis:

  • Examined where research was being published, analyzed the number of outputs in different research areas, and identified trends.
  • Assessed publication frequency in CABS-ranked journals (4* to 1) and compared these to non-CABS journals.
  • Analyzed citation data and field citation ratios to evaluate the impact of publications in different journal ranks.
  • Investigated the academic age of researchers to determine if early career researchers tend to publish in lower-ranked journals.
  • Analyzed total and recent citations to understand publication impact.
  • Compared altmetric scores to gauge the attention received by publications.
  • Examined research outputs categorized by Fields of Research (FOR).
  • Evaluated contributions to SDGs.

Altmetric Data:

  • Explored the use of the Altmetrics API to supplement our analysis with additional metrics, although this was secondary to the main analysis.

Code Development:

  • Developed Python scripts to filter and query the required data using the Dimcli library.
  • Wrote R scripts for data analysis and used Quarto for presenting the findings.

Technologies Used:

  • Python: For querying and mining data from Dimensions.ai using the Dimcli library.
  • R: For detailed data analysis.
  • Quarto: For creating and presenting the final report.

Challenges:

  • Managing large datasets and ensuring efficient querying with the Dimcli library.
  • Combining data from multiple sources and maintaining data integrity throughout the analysis process.

Dashboard Development:

  • Developed the user interface with shiny and fluidPage.
  • Used ggplot2 and plotly for creating interactive visualizations.
  • Implemented data tables using DT for easy data exploration.

Features:

  • Total vs Recent Citations: Visual comparison of total and recent citations for selected universities.
  • Altmetric Score Comparison: Median altmetric scores visualized to understand publication impact.
  • Research Output in Fields: Research outputs categorized by Fields of Research at different levels.
  • SDG Contributions: Number of publications contributing to various SDGs.
  • Benchmarking Universities: Comparative table showing selected metrics for each university.

Outcome:

The report provided valuable insights into the research outputs of the School of Management, highlighting trends, impact metrics and publication practices. This information is crucial for strategic planning and enhancing the research profile of the University.

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) in toddlers

In this above project, I used machine learning to help identify Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) in toddlers based on data from a Q-Chat-10 survey. The goal was to improve early diagnosis and support timely intervention. Using the Design Science Research Methodology (DSRM), I developed a model that classifies toddlers as having ASD symptoms or not, based on their responses to specific questions and demographic information.

To understand which features were most important for the model, I created a bar chart showing the significance of each feature. Here's a quick rundown of the top predictors:

  • A2 (Most Important): Whether the child points to show things.
  • A9: Whether the child looks at the speaker's mouth when they talk.
  • A7 & A8: Whether the child uses simple gestures like waving or nodding, and whether they copy words or sounds spontaneously.
  • A4: Whether the child shows interest in other children.
  • A6: Whether the child plays with toys functionally.
  • A5: Whether the child demonstrates imaginative play.
  • A1: Whether the child responds to their name being called.

By highlighting these key features, my model aims to provide valuable insights into early ASD detection, potentially making a significant impact on children's lives.

AW-Deep-Racer-Student-League

The AWS DeepRacer Student League is where you compete for the fastest lap times with friends, training a reinforcement learning (RL) model and see a simulation of how that model performs on a track.

This project includes a reward function used in training an autonomous car with reinforcement learning. The function rewards the car based on its proximity to the center of the track, with higher rewards for staying closer to the center. Here's a brief overview of how the function works:

  • The track width and distance from the center are read from the input parameters.
  • Three markers are calculated at varying distances from the center line (10%, 25%, and 50% of the track width).
  • The car receives the highest reward if it stays within the first marker, a lower reward if within the second marker and the lowest reward if within the third marker.
  • If the car is beyond the third marker, it receives a minimal reward, indicating it's likely off track or crashed.
