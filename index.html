Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) in toddlers

In this above project, I used machine learning to help identify Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) in toddlers based on data from a Q-Chat-10 survey. The goal was to improve early diagnosis and support timely intervention. Using the Design Science Research Methodology (DSRM), I developed a model that classifies toddlers as having ASD symptoms or not, based on their responses to specific questions and demographic information.

To understand which features were most important for the model, I created a bar chart showing the significance of each feature. Here's a quick rundown of the top predictors:

  • A2 (Most Important): Whether the child points to show things.
  • A9: Whether the child looks at the speaker's mouth when they talk.
  • A7 & A8: Whether the child uses simple gestures like waving or nodding, and whether they copy words or sounds spontaneously.
  • A4: Whether the child shows interest in other children.
  • A6: Whether the child plays with toys functionally.
  • A5: Whether the child demonstrates imaginative play.
  • A1: Whether the child responds to their name being called.

By highlighting these key features, my model aims to provide valuable insights into early ASD detection, potentially making a significant impact on children's lives.

AW-Deep-Racer-Student-League

The AWS DeepRacer Student League is where you compete for the fastest lap times with friends, training a reinforcement learning (RL) model and see a simulation of how that model performs on a track.

This project includes a reward function used in training an autonomous car with reinforcement learning. The function rewards the car based on its proximity to the center of the track, with higher rewards for staying closer to the center. Here's a brief overview of how the function works:

  • The track width and distance from the center are read from the input parameters.
  • Three markers are calculated at varying distances from the center line (10%, 25%, and 50% of the track width).
  • The car receives the highest reward if it stays within the first marker, a lower reward if within the second marker, and the lowest reward if within the third marker.
  • If the car is beyond the third marker, it receives a minimal reward, indicating it's likely off track or crashed.

For more details on the code and visuals on my models performance, visit my GitHub repository.
